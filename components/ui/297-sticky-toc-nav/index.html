Roots & foundations

Every planting starts below the surface, where nothing is visible yet. Roots take hold slowly, and the plant above ground only looks steady because of the unseen work underneath.

Spend time here before rushing toward what shows. A shallow start rarely holds up once the weather turns.

Reading the soil

Good soil tells you what it needs if you're willing to look closely — texture, drainage, the smell after rain. None of it is complicated, but it does take attention.
