Intro

Every plot starts with a plan — which beds, which crops, and how much time you actually have on weekends. This section sets the stage before we get into specifics.

Soil

Good soil is layered, not flat: organic matter on top, structure underneath. A simple jar test tells you more than most soil calculators.

Water

Deep, infrequent watering builds stronger roots than a daily light sprinkle. Mornings are kinder to your plants than midday heat.

Light

Six hours of direct sun is the rough line between "full sun" and "partial shade" crops. Track a spot for a day before committing beds to it.

Pests

Most pest problems are caught early by just looking closely, a few minutes a day, rather than treating on a fixed schedule.

Harvest

Picking regularly tends to encourage more production — plants that go to seed early often slow down for the rest of the season.
