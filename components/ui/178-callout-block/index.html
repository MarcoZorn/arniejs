Callout Block

Four variants: info, warning, danger, tip

Info

Soil temperature matters more than air temperature when deciding when to direct-sow. Use a probe thermometer at a two-inch depth before you trust the calendar.

Warning

Fresh, unfinished compost can burn tender seedlings. Let a hot pile cure for at least three weeks before working it into a bed you plan to sow immediately.

Danger

Never compost diseased nightshade foliage — blight spores survive most home piles and will reinfect next season's tomatoes and potatoes. Bag it for municipal pickup instead.

Tip

Plant marigolds along the border of a vegetable bed — their roots release compounds that deter root-knot nematodes while the blooms bring in pollinators.
