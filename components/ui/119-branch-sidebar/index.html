Roots

Roots anchor the plant and pull water and nutrients up from the soil. They grow slowly, out of sight, but everything above ground depends on what happens down here.

Some roots go deep, chasing groundwater. Others spread wide, close to the surface, catching every bit of rain before it drains away.

Stem

The stem carries water and sugars between roots and leaves, and holds the whole plant upright against wind and weight.

Woody stems build rings year over year; soft stems trade strength for speed, racing toward the light.

Leaves

Leaves are the plant's solar panels, converting light into sugar through photosynthesis, one cell at a time.

Their shape, size, and arrangement are all tuned to the amount of light and water a plant expects to get.

Flowers

Flowers exist for one purpose: reproduction. Color, scent, and shape all evolved to attract exactly the right pollinator.

A flower is a brief, showy negotiation between a plant and an insect, bird, or the wind itself.

Fruit

Fruit is the plant's strategy for moving its seeds somewhere new — wrapped in something worth eating.

By the time fruit ripens, the whole cycle is nearly ready to begin again.
